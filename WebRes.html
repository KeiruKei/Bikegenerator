Power to the Pedal:
Ride the Energy Wave with Bike-Powered Generator

It attempts to improve cycling overall by offering a dependable and portable way to charge different gadgets or accessories while out and about.

It achieves this by using some existing technology and mixing them together to create another technology with features that are able to produce electricity just by cycling.

An In-Depth Analysis of Each Component in The Bike Powered Generator System

Clip Fan Motor
Functions as a generator, converting mechanical energy from the bike cog into electrical power.

Bike
Employs the rider's pedaling motion to drive the bike crank, initiating the energy production process and serving as the primary source of kinetic energy.

Bike Chain
Efficiently transfers rotational energy from the bike crank, linking it to the bike cog to transmit kinetic energy smoothly.

Bike Crank
Initiates and converts physical pedaling effort into rotational kinetic energy, serving as the starting point for the energy production process.

Bike Cog
Precisely receives and transfers rotational energy from the bike chain to the clip fan motor, ensuring effective energy transfer.

Extension Outlet
Serves as a connection point for electrical devices, enabling the directed flow of generated electricity to various appliances.

Charger
Converts the generated electrical power into a format suitable for charging electronic devices, making the energy produced by pedaling usable for gadgets and batteries.

In an era where the bicycle has become an important source of sustainable and accessible transportation, a group of researchers has turned their attention towards a common challenge faced by cyclists: keeping their mobile devices powered on the go. The idea is straightforward: integrating generators into bicycles to offer a reliable energy source. Bicycles have gained popularity for their affordability, health benefits, flexibility, and convenience. However, with modern lifestyles relying heavily on mobile devices for connectivity and productivity, the concept of a bike-powered generator is more than a novelty; it's a potentially transformative solution for cyclists.
